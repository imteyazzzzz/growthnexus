Cookie Policy

Last Updated: July 4, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Growth Nexus uses cookies and local browser storage to optimize site loading, memorize user consent, and enable administrative dashboards.

1. What are Cookies and Local Storage?

Cookies are tiny files stored on your computer by the browser. Local Storage and Session Storage are modern browser features that allow websites to save small text keys on your device. These keys persist until cleared manually or expired automatically.

2. Cookies and Storage Used on Our Site

  • gn_cookies (Cookie Consent): Stores your choice (accepted/declined) when you interact with our Cookie Banner, preventing the banner from popping up again.
  • gn_exit_dismissed (Exit Intent): Tracks if you dismissed the special audit offer popup so that we do not disrupt your browsing session again.
  • gn_leads (Local Leads Cache): Saves a temporary backup of leads submitted from your device. This serves as local history and is used to feed the Leads Dashboard.
  • gn_admin_authenticated (Admin Mode): Stored in Session Storage. It remembers if you have authenticated with the correct admin password, keeping the dashboard unlocked across tab refreshes during your session.

Security Policy

Last Updated: July 4, 2026

At Growth Nexus, security is engineered into every component of our automation workflows. This document details the policies we use to keep user inputs and systems safe.

1. End-to-End Encryption

All data exchanged between our site forms and the Google Sheets integration is transmitted using HTTPS TLS 1.3 encryption protocols. This prevents eavesdropping or interception of customer names, email addresses, or phone details during transmission.

2. Authentication & Admin Security

The Leads Dashboard is secured behind an admin authorization lock requiring a custom security key. Authentication tokens are handled strictly within the browser's Session Storage, meaning once the browser tab is closed, access is automatically revoked to prevent unauthorized access on shared devices.

3. Third-Party API Credentials

All Apps Script integrations connecting to external sheet servers or the Google Tasks API utilize secure, scoped OAuth 2.0 access credentials. Growth Nexus does not store or hardcode private Google password keys in plaintext.
